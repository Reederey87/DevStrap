_Date: 2026-07-10 · Trunk audited: `d667530` (`docs: unattended-operation wave close-out (PRs #136-#139) (#140)`)_

## How this relates to the prior audits

This is the **seventh** design & implementation pass. It does **not** restate the still-open recommendations from PASS4/PASS5; those remain tracked in `docs/audits/README.md` (mapped in **Appendix A**). Pass 6 is fully closed (43/43). This pass concentrates on the large wave of code that shipped *after* Pass 6 closed — roughly PRs #95–#140 — because that is where fresh review adds the most value:

1. **The zero-infrastructure hub carriers (AD-1).** `devstrap hub init <git-url>` now makes a private git repo the **default** quickstart hub (PRs #96–#106), with a local-folder/cloud-drive carrier alongside it. These are a new storage substrate with a different concurrency and durability profile than R2, and they are audited here as production code.
2. **The OS-enforced agent sandbox (P4-GIT-03, shipped complete).** macOS Seatbelt + Linux bubblewrap/Landlock/seccomp + read-confinement + `sandbox.violation` telemetry (PRs #107–#129). A security-critical subsystem gets its first dedicated adversarial pass.
3. **The multi-device completeness wave.** Synced device-trust propagation (`TRUST-01`, #132), cross-device env-profile exchange (`ENV-SYNC-01`, #131), self-healing WCK rotation after revoke (#137), and the draft-pending quarantine (#136) — all new event-plane behavior interacting with the shipped snapshot-exchange/compaction layer.
4. **The distribution & unattended-operation wave.** v0.1.0/v0.1.1 releases (Homebrew cask, `curl|sh`, cosign/SLSA/SBOM), `devstrap service install` (launchd/systemd, #139), and worktree GC (#138).
5. **Commercial readiness.** A managed-hub tier is being planned (`spec/20_COMMERCIALIZATION_AND_PRICING.md`). This pass makes its engineering prerequisites concrete rather than leaving them under the `SCALE-*` "documented-not-built" umbrella.

**ID scheme.** Every finding is prefixed `P7-` so its ID is globally unique, per the ledger convention. Dimension codes: `SEC` (security/crypto/sandbox), `SYNC` (sync engine/convergence/trust), `HUB` (cloud hub/carriers/scale), `GIT` (git materialization/worktrees/agents), `CLI` (CLI/UX), `DATA` (data model/SQLite/backup), `QUAL` (quality/testing/CI/release), `XP` (cross-platform/service/ignore/scan), `DOC` (specs/docs/process), `PROD` (product/commercial readiness).

## Methodology

Findings were produced by a verification-driven multi-agent workflow against the `d667530` worktree. **Ten dimension reviewers** (a mix of Claude opus-4.8, GPT-5.6 via Codex, and Grok-4.5, chosen per dimension) were each told exactly which PASS4/PASS5 IDs are *shipped* vs *open* so they hunt **new** issues; extensions of open items are cross-referenced, never re-issued. Then **every candidate was independently adversarially verified** by a separate opus-4.8 agent that re-opened the cited code, tried to refute it, corrected severity, and checked it against the full open backlog and the work log (which lags the ledger). **Every candidate P1 additionally received a second, independent GPT-5.6 verification pass.** Severities below reflect the verifiers' rulings, not the reviewers' first instinct: three candidate P1s were downgraded to P2 by cross-checking verifiers (the notarization footgun is dormant and documented; the installer gap is defense-in-depth against a scoped-token compromise; the worktree-cleanup race is caught in the common case by git's own dirty check), leaving one confirmed P1. External best-practice anchors (six exa-backed research topics) are cited inline and collected in **Appendix B**.

Where two dimensions surfaced the same defect, it is stated once under a primary ID with a cross-reference: the headline P1 was independently found by the SYNC, DATA, and SEC reviewers *and* confirmed by both the opus and GPT-5.6 verifiers, tracing the identical code path four times over.

**Severity:** P1 = correctness/security/data-loss or major; P2 = significant; P3 = minor/polish/DX. **Effort:** S ≈ <½ day, M ≈ 1–3 days, L ≈ ~1 week, XL ≈ multi-week.

## Executive summary

The post-Pass-6 waves are a real advance: the git-carrier hub removes the last infrastructure prerequisite, the OS sandbox closes the agent-escape gap that had been open since Pass 4, and the multi-device trust/env waves make a fleet genuinely converge. This pass found the subsystem that ties them together — **event-log compaction plus snapshot recovery — silently discards device-revocation state**, and a scatter of P2 durability, release-integrity, and hosted-readiness gaps around the new surfaces.

Risk clusters in five themes:

1. **Revocation does not survive compaction (`P7-SYNC-01`, the one P1).** Device-trust (`device.revoked`/`device.lost`) propagates only as a live event. The `snapshot.v1` object omits device trust entirely; `hub compact` advances the retention floor from the compactor's *own* transport cursors (gated only by its own completeness, never by other devices' acks) and deletes the revoke event below the floor; a device that was offline across the revoke-then-compact window is forced into snapshot recovery, advances its cursor past the deleted event, and **permanently keeps the revoked — possibly compromised — device as `approved`**, accepting its signed events forever with no self-heal. This defeats the entire point of revocation for any device that missed the window. It was found by three independent reviewers and confirmed by two independent verifiers.

2. **The new hub carriers have durability edges R2 doesn't.** The git carrier treats a deleted/rewound carrier branch as a fresh empty hub and re-founds it, silently losing any hub state no currently-online device re-pushes (`P7-HUB-02`); its local clone and observed-key index grow unbounded because nothing runs `git gc` or prunes after compaction (`P7-HUB-03`); and the folder/cloud-drive carrier writes its mutable retention manifest with a non-atomic `os.WriteFile`, so a torn write fail-closes every device's sync (`P7-HUB-05`).

3. **Backup/restore and post-revoke containment are not crash-atomic.** `db backup --full` enumerates blob refs from the live store *after* the `VACUUM INTO` snapshot, so a concurrent rotation can make the archive omit a referenced blob with no warning (`P7-DATA-03`); restore validates only `state.db`, not the keys/blobs a recovery actually needs (`P7-DATA-04`), and a mid-promotion crash leaves a mixed-generation home (`P7-DATA-05`). Post-revoke WCK rotation and the needs-rotation flag run *outside* the trust-flip transaction with no durable resume marker, so a crash leaves the fleet sealing under an epoch the revoked device still holds (`P7-SEC-02`).

4. **The release and sandbox edges are honest-but-incomplete.** The `curl|sh` installer verifies only sha256 and never touches the cosign/SLSA artifacts it ships (`P7-QUAL-02`, top P2); the macOS notarization block activates on one of its five secrets, risking signed-but-not-notarized artifacts before Homebrew's 2026-09-01 Gatekeeper deadline (`P7-QUAL-03`); the sandbox credential deny-list omits `~/.git-credentials` and cloud-CLI token stores (`P7-SEC-01`); and `--sandbox require` fails closed for network and read-confine but *not* for credential reads on the Landlock fallback that is the default modern-Ubuntu path (`P7-SEC-03`).

5. **The managed tier is a plan without its prerequisites, and the docs claim shipped work as unbuilt.** There is no control-plane identity binding a `workspace_id` to an account, no server-side lifecycle/quota enforcement (the operator holds no keys), and no version-skew policy for a `brew`-upgraded fleet (`P7-PROD-02/03/04`). Meanwhile six files still describe the shipped `devstrap service` and OS sandbox as future work (`P7-DOC-01`), and the audit ledger's own Pass-5 count is stale (`P7-DOC-02`).

The near-term imperative: **carry terminal device-trust in the snapshot and re-derive it on import** (`P7-SYNC-01`) before any fleet relies on compaction for revocation; **make backup/restore and post-revoke containment crash-atomic** (`P7-DATA-03/04/05`, `P7-SEC-02`); **harden the two new carriers' durability** (`P7-HUB-02/05`); and **close the installer/notarization release gaps** (`P7-QUAL-02/03`) ahead of the Homebrew deadline.

## Security, Crypto & Sandbox

**P7-SEC-01 · P2 · S · Sandbox credential deny-list omits `.git-credentials` and cloud-CLI token stores.**
`internal/platform/sandbox_profile.go:7,13` define the sole deny set (`.ssh`, `.aws`, `.gnupg`, `.config/gh`, `.kube`, `.docker`; files `.netrc`, `.npmrc`, `.pypirc`, `.gitconfig`) that feeds both the Seatbelt profile and the bubblewrap args. `~/.git-credentials` (git's plaintext `credential.helper store` file), `~/.config/gcloud` (GCP refresh tokens), and `~/.azure` are absent. Under the default `--policy guarded` (allow-default reads, open network), a compromised agent child reads them by absolute path and exfiltrates every stored git HTTPS token / cloud credential — the `.gitconfig` that *is* masked merely points at the `.git-credentials` that isn't. **Fix:** add the three paths to the single list; `credentialAnchors`/`readConfineRoots`/tests inherit it. Update the enumerations in `spec/10:141` and `spec/15`.

**P7-SEC-02 · P2 · S · Post-revoke containment runs outside the trust-flip transaction and is lost on a crash.** (folds in the DATA reviewer's rotation-flag finding.)
`internal/cli/devices.go:324-332` commits the trust flip + signed `device.revoked` event in one `WithTx`. Everything that *contains* the revoked device runs afterward as separate best-effort calls: `rotateWorkspaceKeyOnRevoke` (:360), `MarkEncryptedBindingsNeedingRotation` (:361), `rewrapBlobsOnRevoke` (:382). The owed-rotation marker is written **only** in the `Rotate`-error branch (:828); the `CurrentEpoch`-error path (:810-812) returns with no marker, and a SIGKILL/ctx-cancel between the tx commit and `Rotate` leaves the trust flipped with no containment record. `sync.go` only retries rotation when `pending||aged` (default 90d), so the fleet keeps sealing under the epoch the revoked device holds, with no `doctor` warning. The origin can't self-heal by pulling its own event back — `InsertEvent` dedups before `applyEventTx` (`events.go:535-536`). **Fix:** persist a `revoke_containment_pending` marker in the same `WithTx` as the trust flip; clear it only after the post-revoke sequence succeeds; have `sync`/`doctor` resume it.

**P7-SEC-03 · P3 · S · `--sandbox require` does not fail closed on credential reads under the Landlock fallback.**
`internal/cli/agent.go` gates `require` to refuse when network-deny (:187-189) or read-confine (:209-210) cannot be enforced, but there is **no** analogous gate for the always-requested `DenySensitiveReads`. The Landlock fallback cannot subtract read access (`sandbox_landlock.go:93-96`) and drops the credential deny, surfacing it only as a `notice:` line (`agent.go:196-199`). Landlock is the default path on modern Ubuntu (23.10+, where AppArmor breaks bubblewrap's user namespaces), so `agent run --sandbox require` there launches with `~/.ssh`/`~/.aws`/`~/.git-credentials` fully readable — the confinement the user explicitly demanded. `spec/15:91` calls `require` "the fail-closed mode" without qualification. **Fix:** under `require`, either fail closed on the credential deny or auto-engage read-confine (which Landlock *can* enforce and which subsumes credential paths); qualify the spec claim. (Verifier note: the reviewer's "silently absent" overstates it — a notice *is* emitted; the defect is the fail-closed asymmetry, not silence.)

**P7-SEC-04 · P3 · M · Folder/cloud-drive carrier symlink confinement is check-then-use (TOCTOU).**
`internal/hub/gitcarrier.go:691-709` `safePath` `Lstat`-walks components then returns a path that callers open separately with no `O_NOFOLLOW` (`os.ReadFile`/`os.WriteFile` at :829/:817/:992). The git carrier is safe (private checkout, static symlinks, tested), but the folder carrier roots this store directly in the shared drive (`folder.go:115`) and `revalidateRoot` re-checks only the root. On an SMB/NFS carrier a co-user who swaps an intermediate component for a symlink to `~/.ssh` between the `Lstat` and the `WriteFile` makes the victim write a devstrap object outside the carrier root. **Fix:** `O_NOFOLLOW` on the final component + `openat`-style parent-fd resolution; or document the folder-carrier TOCTOU explicitly as a residual.

**P7-SEC-05 · P3 · S · `TRUST-01` gives one compromised approved device a fleet-wide revocation + rotation-lockout DoS (threat-model doc gap).**
`internal/cli/devices.go:316-333`: any approved device can emit `device.revoked`/`device.lost` for every peer, each of which flips the target to revoked and rotates the WCK excluding it — with no quorum, rate-limit, or human confirmation, and re-approval is a per-device local fingerprint ceremony. Before `TRUST-01` (c49e090) revocation was local-only; the blast-radius expansion is undocumented. The mechanism is *correct* (authenticity/monotonicity/rotation gating all verified sound); this is a self-inflicted-DoS surface, best resolved as a `spec/15` blast-radius note (and optionally a revocation rate-limit / local-quarantine-vs-propagated split), not a code bug.

## Sync Engine, Convergence & Trust

**P7-SYNC-01 · P1 · L · Device revocation is erased by compaction and absent from snapshots, so a snapshot-recovering device permanently keeps a revoked device approved.** (canonical; independently found by the SYNC, DATA, and SEC reviewers; confirmed by the opus and GPT-5.6 verifiers.)
All four sub-conditions were verified against code:
- (a) `internal/sync/snapshot.go:88-99` — the `Snapshot` struct is `Entries`/`Tombstones`/`Anchors`/`Floor`/`HLC` only; the comment deliberately excludes device-local state, and `snapshot_build.go`/`snapshot_import.go` neither read nor apply device trust. `device.revoked`/`device.lost` is the *only* path that derives synced terminal trust (`events.go:916-955`).
- (b) `internal/cli/hub_compact.go:449-468` `computeCompactFloors` sets `floors[dev]=cursor+1` from the compactor's **own** transport cursors, gated only by `refuseIfIncompleteView` (its own completeness). `CompactEventsBelow` (:302) then deletes the revoke event — which lives in the revoker's own origin stream — below the floor (R2 deletion at `internal/hub/r2.go:707-789`, and `:856-887` can delete the revoked device's whole stream).
- (c) `internal/cli/sync.go:107-112` → `snapshot_recovery.go:185` → `snapshot_import.go:67-74` advances the pull cursor to `floor-1`, past the deleted revoke, with **no** trust re-derivation.
- (d) The all-device-ack completeness clock gates tombstone-GC only, **not** `computeCompactFloors`; there is no revoke re-emission on approval (only WCK grants re-emit).

**Scenario:** fleet A,B,C,D all approved; D goes offline; B revokes A (the event reaches B,C but not D); a compactor advances the floor past the revoke and deletes it; D returns, its cursor for the revoker's stream is below the floor, it imports the snapshot, keeps A `approved`, and accepts A's future signed events indefinitely. Permanent, silent trust split; a repeated operator revoke can re-emit but nothing signals that it's needed. **Fix:** add `Trust []SnapshotTrust{DeviceID, State, SourceEventHLC, SourceEventDeviceID, SourceEventID}` to the snapshot; populate it in `BuildSnapshot` from `devices` rows in `revoked`/`lost`; re-apply in `ImportSnapshot` via `ApplyRemoteDeviceTrustTx` (never flip local; flag `needs_rotation` on an actual change) in the same transaction as namespace state; bump the snapshot version so older clients fail closed rather than silently ignore the new projection. Add an e2e: approve B on C → C offline → revoke B → compact → C snapshot-recovers → B stays rejected. **Correct** the `spec/07` and ledger `TRUST-01` claims that revocation is unqualifiedly "fleet-wide" — it holds only for devices online across the revoke-to-compaction window.

**P7-SYNC-02 · P3 · M · `TRUST-01` makes namespace convergence delivery-order-dependent for a revoked device's pre-revocation events.**
`internal/state/store.go:~3313` `verifyEventSignature` rejects on the *current* `trustState != "approved"` with no comparison of the event's HLC to the revoke's, and no `revoked_at_hlc` is recorded. A legitimate `project.*` event `e1@HLC50` from A, applied on B before B revokes A `@HLC100`, is *rejected* when it reaches bystander C only after C applied the revoke — so B keeps the update and C never gets it. Permanent divergence by delivery interleaving, more reachable on the batched git carrier; recovery is re-approving A. `spec/07:~616` documents this only for *mutual* revocation. **Fix:** record `devices.revoked_at_hlc`; reject a revoked device's event only when `event.HLC >= revoked_at_hlc`; else document the divergence class.

**P7-SYNC-03 · P3 · S · Draft-snapshot "latest" selection uses non-deterministic local tiebreakers.**
`internal/state/store.go:1997-2007` `LatestDraftSnapshot` orders by `source_event_hlc DESC, created_at DESC, id DESC` — but `created_at` is a local timestamp and `id` is a locally-minted `snap_<uuidv7>`, both differing per device for the same source event. The canonical fleet tiebreak everywhere else is `(hlc, source_event_device_id, source_event_id)` (`events.go:1228` `samePathLess`); the env plane already uses `envCoordLess`. On an HLC tie, two devices materialize/GC different bundles. **Fix:** `ORDER BY source_event_hlc DESC, source_event_device_id DESC, source_event_id DESC`.

**P7-SYNC-04 · P3 · M · Self-healing WCK rotation is owed only by the local revoker; a fleet learning via synced trust never closes the epoch.**
`internal/sync/events.go:947-955` — a remote `device.revoked`/`device.lost` apply flips trust and flags `needs_rotation` but never sets `wck_rotation_pending` nor triggers a rotation; the pending marker is written only by the local revoke path (`devices.go:828`). If the local revoker's `Rotate` fails and it then goes permanently offline, every other device knows A is revoked yet none owes a rotation, so the fleet keeps sealing under the epoch A holds — forward secrecy never re-established. Distinct code path from `P7-SEC-02` (remote apply vs local crash); complementary facets of the #134 self-healing wave. **Fix:** when a remote trust event actually flips a row, record `wck_rotation_pending` on the receiver so its own sync rotation gate self-heals (its `Rotate` excludes the revoked device); storm-guard like the age-rotate gate.

## Cloud Hub & Carriers

**P7-HUB-02 · P2 · M · The git carrier has no head-continuity guard, so a branch deletion or backward force-push is silently accepted as a fresh carrier → partial hub-state loss.**
`internal/hub/gitcarrier.go:224-238` — `refreshLocked` on `ErrBranchNotFound` clears the working tree and treats the carrier as brand-new; `validateMarkerLocked` (:249-279) only refuses a *non-empty* tree lacking the marker, and `fetchedSHA` is in-memory only, so nothing persists that the carrier previously had a non-empty head. Aggravated by the push watermark (`sync.go:468-480`), which never re-pushes events already below a device's cursor. A force-push / branch-delete / backup-restore of the carrier repo makes each device re-found it and push only its local delta; hub objects that no currently-online device re-pushes (offline devices' blobs, events below every push cursor) are permanently lost, silently. Not covered by the carrier's adversarial tests. **Fix:** persist a last-known-good head beside the clone; refuse to re-found on an absent/non-descendant head after a non-empty observation, with a "carrier history was rewritten — investigate" error and a documented peer/snapshot recovery.

**P7-HUB-01 · P3 · M · The git carrier does one fetch+commit+push per hub object, not per sync cycle.**
`gitcarrier.go:322` `writeLoop` (lock → fetch → apply → commit → push) is invoked per Hub method, and `sync.go:503-524` `pushReferencedBlobs` fans out one `PutBlob` per blob, each serialized on the carrier lock — so a cycle with B blobs is `B+2` serialized push cycles and `B+2` new carrier commits. The header's "one commit for the whole batch" is true only per Hub call. On the *default* quickstart hub this inflates carrier history between compactions and, under concurrent multi-device pushes, drives non-fast-forward retry churn that can exhaust `gitPushAttempts=5`. (Verifier downgraded P2→P3: pure performance/scale, no data loss; edges to P2 only if concurrent multi-device sync on one carrier is common.) **Fix:** batch a whole cycle's hub writes into one carrier commit+push via a batch/`Flush` seam. External anchor: git-annex treats non-fast-forward rejection as the normal, retryable outcome of concurrent pushes (Appendix B, T1).

**P7-HUB-03 · P3 · M · Carrier local caches grow unbounded — the clone is never `git gc`'d and `observed.json` never prunes compacted keys.** (folds in the host-GC-lag finding.)
No `git gc`/`maintenance`/`repack`/`prune` runs anywhere in `gitcarrier.go`; after `CompactEventsBelow`'s parentless-squash force-push, old objects are unreachable in the local clone but never pruned, and `forgetObservedLocked` fires only from `DeleteObject`, so remote compaction (via `reset --hard`) never prunes `observed.json`. Over months the per-device clone and index grow without bound though the live hub stays small; the header's "the host garbage-collects the unreachable objects" is remote-only, and even remotely, GitHub/GitLab defer GC and count unreachable objects toward the repo size limit — a busy carrier can approach the host's 5 GB limit and wedge all pushes with no warning (the folded host-GC-lag half; dedup vs `P4-HUB-15` for the monitoring piece). **Fix:** run `git gc --prune=now`/`git maintenance run` on the clone after compaction and periodically; prune `observed.json` to the live key set; document the host-GC-lag caveat in `spec/07`/`spec/19` and add a `doctor --remote` carrier-growth probe. External anchor: a git-annex tracking branch grew to 5 GB vs 40 MB of content without scheduled repacking (Appendix B, T1).

**P7-HUB-05 · P3 · S · The folder carrier writes objects with a non-atomic `os.WriteFile`, so a torn retention manifest fail-closes every sync.**
`gitcarrier.go:817,992` `fsObjectStore.PutObject`/`PutObjectIfMatch` write in place with no temp-file+rename. The git carrier is shielded (private clone, `reset --hard` discards torn pre-commit files); the folder carrier writes straight into the shared/replicated folder (`folder.go:115`). A crash between truncate and full write — or a cloud drive (Dropbox/iCloud/OneDrive) replicating mid-write — leaves a partial `retention.json`, and `retentionFloors` fail-closes every device's `Pull` on the parse error (`r2.go:565-569` → :270-273). Content-addressed blobs/events are hash-safe; the single-key mutable manifest and `sweep.lock` are the exposure. **Fix:** write to a temp file in the same dir, `fsync`, then `os.Rename` (atomic on one filesystem); document that rename reduces but doesn't eliminate the cloud-drive mid-replication window. External anchor: `VACUUM INTO`/`.backup` and atomic-rename are the standard safe-write patterns; naive in-place copies are explicitly unsafe (Appendix B, T6).

## Git Materialization & Agents

**P7-GIT-01 · P2 · M · `worktree cleanup --merged` can reap a worktree an agent is actively running in, with a TOCTOU window and no `agent_runs` correlation.** (candidate P1, downgraded by both the opus and GPT-5.6 verifiers.)
`internal/cli/worktree.go:456-566` — the cleanup loop never queries `agent_runs` for a running row on the worktree (the store has `RunningAgentRunsWithPID` but no by-worktree query and cleanup calls neither). It checks `DirtyState` once (:508), then does a network fetch (:520), `branch --merged`/`IsSquashMerged` (:525-533, reachable for an in-use worktree), and `WorktreeRemove` + `git branch -D` (:542-548) with no re-check and no repo lock held across any of it. `IsSquashMerged` (`internal/git/git.go:506-545`) returns true whenever the branch's tree currently equals the base — true for a freshly created, still-running agent branch. **Downgrade rationale (verifier consensus):** `WorktreeRemove` omits `--force` by default, so git refuses if uncommitted changes appear before removal, catching the common editing case; `cleanup --merged` is a manual command (not run by `run-loop`), so the race needs a concurrent human/teammate; the true data-loss path is the documented coincidental-identical-diff limitation newly applied to *live* worktrees. Real gap, P1 overstates the blast radius. **Fix:** query `agent_runs` for a running row (after PID reconciliation) and skip; re-check `DirtyState` immediately before removal; fold the decision+action under `acquireRepoLock`.

**P7-GIT-02 · P2 · S · `worktree cleanup` mutates worktrees/branches outside the repo lock, unlike every other git-mutating path.**
`worktree.go:508-548` runs `DirtyState`/`WorktreeRemove`/`branch -D` lock-free; the repo lock is scoped only to the base-ref fetch inside `refreshWorktreeBase` (:568-579). `createFreshWorktree` and `materializeGitRepo` hold the lock across their entire flow by contrast. A concurrent `worktree new --fresh-upstream` and a `cleanup` on the same project mutate `.git/worktrees/` admin state and refs simultaneously — exactly the interleaving `acquireRepoLock` exists to serialize. Subsumed by `P7-GIT-01`'s lock fix; flag overlap. **Fix:** hold the project repo lock across the dirty-check-through-removal sequence.

**P7-GIT-03 · P2 · M · `processAlive` has no PID-reuse guard, so repo-lock staleness and the agent-run sweep can wedge permanently after a PID is recycled.**
`internal/cli/repo_lock.go:193-198` — `processAlive` is `FindProcess` + `Signal(0)` with no start-time/identity check. It backs both `repoLockIsStale` (:150-157, where liveness is authoritative over age, so a recycled-PID collision defeats the age fallback too) and `sweepStaleAgentRuns` (`agent_sweep.go`), which gates `agent pr`'s completeness check (`P6-GIT-06`). A crashed holder whose PID is recycled by an unrelated long-lived process (common under this codebase's own fork-heavy git/LFS/forge workloads) reads as alive: the repo lock refuses forever and the crashed run stays `running` indefinitely. Narrow window. **Fix:** record process start time (Linux `/proc/<pid>/stat` field 22; macOS `proc_pidinfo`/`sysctl KERN_PROC`) alongside the PID and compare on the liveness check; one fix in `processAlive` covers both call sites.

## CLI, UX & Developer Experience

**P7-CLI-01 · P2 · S · `db backup --full --json` and `db restore --json` emit non-JSON warning text before the JSON payload, breaking the stream.**
`internal/cli/db_backup.go:96-106` writes `warning: …` lines with raw `Fprintf(stdout, …)` unconditionally, then `:107` `opts.render` encodes the result struct to the same writer under `--json`; restore has the identical shape (`warnKeychainCustodyRestore` at :383-397 before `render` at :373). Reproduced live: `db backup --full out.tar --json` with a missing blob emits warning lines then JSON, and `json.load` fails at "line 1 column 1". **P7-CLI-02 · P2 · S · `worktree cleanup` is the only worktree subcommand with no `Args` validator, silently swallowing a stray positional on a destructive blanket sweep.**
`worktree.go:459-462` — the `cleanup` command literal has no `Args:` field (defaulting to arbitrary args); every sibling (`unlock`, `new`, `status`, `finalize`, `remove`) sets `usageArgs(...)`. A user who writes `worktree cleanup wt_01jz… --merged` meaning "clean up this one" instead sweeps and `git branch -D`s **every** clean merged worktree fleet-wide, the typed id silently discarded — amplified by `P7-GIT-01`. **Fix:** `Args: usageArgs(cobra.NoArgs)`. (Note: `db migrate/status/down` share this gap but are pre-existing, out of this pass's scope — recorded for a future CLI-consistency sweep.)

**P7-CLI-03 · P3 · S · `hub init` and `service install` route their only success confirmation through `progressf`, so `--quiet` produces no output for a real state-changing action.**
`hub.go:578` `progressf "Configured hub: %s"` is the sole confirmation that `hub init` rewrote `config.yaml`; `service.go:87-91`'s install confirmations are all `progressf` (the notes loop is empty on macOS). Reproduced live: `hub init … --quiet` (exit 0, wrote the hub line, printed nothing) and `service install … --quiet` (exit 0, installed *and started* a LaunchAgent, printed nothing). The `P6-CLI-04` contract is that results still print under `--quiet`; a config mutation and a started background service are results, not progress. **Fix:** print the confirmation unconditionally (raw `Fprintf`), mirroring `service status` and `devices revoke`.

## Data Model, SQLite & Backup

**P7-DATA-03 · P2 · M · `db backup --full` enumerates blob refs from the live store *after* the `VACUUM INTO` snapshot, so a concurrent rotation makes the archive inconsistent.**
`internal/cli/db_backup.go:76` runs `VACUUM INTO` (the archive DB), then `:80` `AllBlobRefs` reads the **live** store, and `:212-228` stages/verifies blobs against that later ref set. A concurrent env rotation (the run-loop or another CLI process on the WAL DB) between the two makes the archive DB hold the old binding while the tar carries the new blob and omits the old — with no missing-blob warning. Restoring yields a valid DB pointing at an uncaptured blob. **Fix:** treat the `VACUUM INTO` DB as the authority for every archive decision — enumerate refs, custody, current device, and held WCK coords from it, not the live store; make a missing referenced blob fatal for `--full`.

**P7-DATA-04 · P2 · M · `db restore` validates SQLite integrity but not archive recoverability.**
`db_backup.go:345-351` requires and validates only `state.db` (`quick_check` + `foreign_key_check`, `store.go:343-360`); `:362-370` restores whichever optional targets happen to be present, `:471-475` treats a missing staged target as a successful no-op, `--full` may omit referenced blobs, and local blob reads don't verify ciphertext against the content-addressed filename. A tar with `state.db` but a missing/corrupt `keys/` or env blob restores "successfully" yet cannot sign, decrypt, or hydrate — and with `--force` replaces a valid live DB with an unrecoverable one. **Fix:** add a versioned archive manifest (required target set, sizes, SHA-256 per entry); before any swap, open the staged DB read-only, enumerate its device/keys/custody/blob-refs, and hash-verify the corresponding staged files; reject incomplete archives.

**P7-DATA-05 · P2 · M · A mid-promotion restore failure leaves a mixed-generation home.**
`db_backup.go:361-370` promotes `state.db`/`config.yaml`/`blobs/`/`keys/` sequentially and returns on the first error; `swapBackupTarget` (:490-491) rolls back only the target currently being swapped and deletes each prior target's `.bak` as soon as *that* rename succeeds. A crash after DB+config promote but during `blobs/` leaves the promoted targets un-rolled-back — combining the restored DB/config with old keys/blobs (dangling refs, custody or WCK mismatch). **Fix:** acquire a state-wide maintenance lock excluding the run-loop during restore; keep all old targets until every new target promotes; roll all back on any error; persist a recovery journal so startup can complete or reverse an interrupted restore. (`P7-DATA-03/04/05` share one fix surface — a versioned manifest + all-or-nothing swap — and should ship as one backup/restore-hardening item, though each is a distinct defect.)

**P7-DATA-06 · P3 · S · Env-sync and rewrap reference scans lack blob-reference indexes.**
`secret_bindings` is indexed only on `env_profile_id` (`00001_initial.sql:195`) and `draft_snapshots` only on `namespace_id` (`00009:18`); the `ENV-SYNC-01` lookup and the bulk rewrap both filter/update the `encrypted_value_ref` column unindexed (`store.go:2243-2251`, :2404-2412), and `blob_gc.go` repeats the lookup per distinct ref. A workspace with N env profiles makes a security-sensitive revoke/rewrap do N unindexed lookups on the single writer — toward quadratic, biting large fleets. Not a duplicate of `P4-SYNC-07` (the single-writer serialization itself). **Fix:** add `secret_bindings(encrypted_value_ref, env_profile_id)` (partial for non-NULL refs) and `draft_snapshots(blob_ref, namespace_id)`; pin with `EXPLAIN QUERY PLAN` tests.

**P7-DATA-07 · P3 · S · Migration 00023 rollback silently destroys env LWW state.**
`internal/state/migrations/00023_env_profile_source_events.sql:8-11` unconditionally drops the three `source_event_*` columns on down; `events.go:897-903` treats absent coordinates as "no incumbent winner," so after `db down` → re-up, a delayed older env event overwrites the newer value. Narrow (needs down/up plus a delayed old event on another origin stream). **Fix:** fail closed before rolling 00023 down when any profile has non-NULL coordinates, unless an explicit destructive override after a backup; add a populated-rollback refusal test.

## Code Quality, Testing & Release

**P7-QUAL-02 · P2 · M · The recommended `curl|sh` installer ignores the cosign signature and SLSA provenance it ships (extends P4-SEC-05).** _(reviewers split P1/P2; adjudicated P2.)_
`scripts/install.sh:65-85` downloads the archive + `checksums.txt` from the same release and verifies **only** sha256; it never fetches `checksums.txt.sigstore.json` or `multiple.intoto.jsonl` and never invokes cosign/`slsa-verifier`, though `.goreleaser.yaml:55-58` emits the sigstore bundle and `release.yml:104-119` emits SLSA provenance. An attacker who can write release assets (a scoped token, without repo write) replaces both tarball and `checksums.txt`, and the installer reports "Checksum verified" and executes the substituted binary. **Adjudication:** the dimension reviewer and the GPT-5.6 P1 pass rated this P1 (arbitrary code execution via an advertised path); the cross-checking verifier rated it P2 because `install.sh` itself is fetched unsigned from mutable `main`, so an attacker with *repo* write bypasses any in-script verifier anyway — cosign therefore closes only the narrower release-asset-only (scoped-token) compromise, making it defense-in-depth rather than a critical hole, and industry-standard `curl|sh` relies on TLS + same-origin checksums. Ranked the top P2. **Fix:** authenticate `checksums.txt` against the pinned release-workflow identity before trusting any hash, then verify the archive against SLSA provenance; fail closed if the verifier is unavailable (with an explicit checksum-only escape hatch); provide a version-pinned/signed installer entry point since the script is fetched from mutable `main`. External anchors: Apple-notarization and supply-chain practice (Appendix B, T5).

**P7-QUAL-03 · P2 · S · The macOS notarize block activates on one of its five secrets, risking signed-but-not-notarized artifacts (extends P4-SEC-05).** _(candidate P1, downgraded by both verifiers.)_
`.goreleaser.yaml:70-79` gates the whole signing/notarization block on `isEnvSet "MACOS_SIGN_P12"` alone while consuming five `MACOS_*` values; `release.yml:89-97` passes all five independently with no all-or-nothing guard; `RELEASING.md:130-132` itself admits that with only `MACOS_SIGN_P12` GoReleaser "signs but silently skips notarization — Gatekeeper still fails." A partial-secret state during Apple enrollment yields Developer-ID-signed-but-unnotarized artifacts, and the cask still relies on its quarantine-strip hook — which Homebrew's 2026-09-01 policy (casks failing Gatekeeper checks are removed from taps; `--no-quarantine` is being removed) will reject on Apple silicon. **Downgrade rationale:** the block is dormant today (no secrets set) and the footgun is documented; it fires only on a future operator mistake. External anchor: OpenAI Codex's cask was rejected in production for being signed-but-not-notarized (Appendix B, T5). **Fix:** a pre-GoReleaser gate accepting exactly 0 or 5 `MACOS_*` values; require all five for template enablement; add a post-build `spctl -a -t install` gate before the tap update; remove the quarantine-strip hook only after it passes.

**P7-QUAL-01 · P2 · L · Stable promotion rebuilds and publishes artifacts that were never smoke-tested as release candidates.**
`RELEASING.md:37-54` describes downloading the rc artifacts as "the exact binaries users will get," then creating a stable tag and re-invoking the workflow (`release.yml:68-76`, a fresh `release --clean` with mutable `version: "~> v2"`), which updates the tap (`.goreleaser.yaml:86-113`) and publishes non-draft (:126-129) before provenance is generated (`release.yml:104-119`). Since rc and stable binaries differ by the injected version string, a tag-specific version bug, toolchain drift, or packaging regression reaches users even though the *tested* binaries were the rc ones. (Verifier note: `release.yml:42` reruns `go test -race` on the stable tag, so *source* validation isn't lost — only artifact-level smoke; defensibly P3.) **Fix:** pin exact GoReleaser + Go patch versions; stage the stable build without updating the tap; smoke-test the stable archives (version/commit/date, completions, checksums, SBOM, cosign) on native macOS/Linux; publish and update the tap only in a final gated job, keeping the release draft until all gates pass.

**P7-QUAL-04 · P2 · M · The shipped `devstrap service` install has no live launchd/systemd end-to-end gate.**
`internal/cli/service_test.go:15-17` uses an in-memory fake; `service_darwin_test.go`/`service_linux_test.go` replace `launchctl`/`systemctl` with PATH shims; `ci.yml` has no service-install smoke step, and `spec/16:470,480` says real `launchctl bootstrap`/systemd-user execution remains manual. A rendered plist/unit stays golden-stable and the mocked argv passes while a real manager rejects the file, installs it into the wrong domain, or starts a run-loop that immediately exits — CI green, unattended convergence silently dead after login/reboot. **Fix:** add a containerized systemd-user smoke and a macOS launchd bootstrap-and-status smoke to CI (even if gated/nightly).

**P7-QUAL-07 · P2 · M · The folder-carrier advisory lock's stale-break has no owner/PID check, so a suspend-mid-write can let two writers hold it.**
`internal/hub/gitcarrier.go:437-438` (folder-carrier lock) stale-breaks purely on mtime with no owner/PID comparison (the PID written at :408 is never read back); `:426-431` refreshes the heartbeat with an unconditional `Chtimes` and `:421-422`/release does an unconditional `os.Remove`. A laptop sleep / SIGSTOP / cgroup freeze longer than the stale threshold freezes the heartbeat goroutine while wall-clock advances, so a second process breaks the lock and both write. The "can never be stolen" comment is provably false. (Verifier note: blast radius is the *local* git/folder hub-cache carrier — re-clonable/re-syncable — not the R2 path; worst case is a lost-then-re-pushed update or a corrupt local checkout, needing the specific suspend-mid-write window.) **Fix:** owner nonce written into the lock + verified on break, plus `flock`/`fcntl` where the filesystem supports it.

**P7-QUAL-05 · P3 · M · A single aggregate coverage floor can mask a package-local regression.**
`ci.yml:118` measures every package (`go test -race -covermode=atomic -coverprofile=coverage.out ./...`) and `:127-134` gates the **aggregate** total at 50% (`P5-QUAL-04`). Because the gate is one repo-wide number, a well-covered package's gains can offset a sharp drop in a specific — possibly security-sensitive — package without tripping the floor; and a package like `cmd/spec-drift` that carries no package-local test contributes nothing that would catch its own regression. **Fix:** add per-package (or per-critical-package) floors alongside the aggregate, and a package-local test for `cmd/spec-drift`.

**P7-QUAL-06 · P3 · S · Two fuzz corpora are never exercised in CI.**
`ci.yml:126` runs only `FuzzDecideConvergence`; `FuzzParseBytes` (`internal/envfile`) and `FuzzCompile` (`internal/ignore`) exist but are never mutation-fuzzed in CI, so parser/ignore regressions only surface if a seed already covers them. **Fix:** add a short fuzz-smoke step for each, matching the Decide pattern.

## Cross-Platform, Service, Ignore & Scan

**P7-XP-01 · P2 · M · `service install` pins the Homebrew Cellar path, so `brew upgrade` silently bricks the unit.**
`internal/cli/service.go:206-226` `resolveServiceExecPath` runs `os.Executable()` + `EvalSymlinks` (refusing only ephemeral `$TMPDIR`/`go-build`), so the `/opt/homebrew/bin/devstrap` symlink resolves into `/opt/homebrew/Cellar/devstrap/<ver>/bin/devstrap`, baked into the plist/unit (`service_launchd.go`/`service_systemd.go`). `brew upgrade` moves the Cellar path; launchd `KeepAlive`/systemd `Restart=on-failure` then thrash every 30 s (exit 127) while `service status` still reports Installed. **Fix:** prefer the stable symlink when the binary lives under `/opt/homebrew/bin`, `/usr/local/bin`, or `~/.local/bin` (don't resolve it), or refuse Cellar paths with a `--exec-path` remedy; document re-running `service install` after upgrades; warn on a missing ExecPath (ties `P7-XP-05`).

**P7-XP-02 · P2 · M · `service install` never checks key custody, so a keychain-custody store strands the unattended run-loop.**
`service.go:46-98` validates only `hubConfigured` + the exec path; `spec.Env` stays nil and no custody `Probe` runs. A store that records `custody=keychain` under a headless systemd unit (no session D-Bus) fails `EnsureSigning` every tick (`sync.go:702-707`), and five consecutive tick failures exit the run-loop into a restart loop. `doctor`'s custody warning isn't invoked at install time, and `spec/06:124` documents the fail-closed behavior but the installer still succeeds silently. **Fix:** at install, read `KeyCustody` and `Probe` under the current session; on Linux require file custody or an explicit opt-in when keychain-custody is detected; note the locked-keychain risk on macOS; fold the check into `doctor` when a service is installed.

**P7-XP-03 · P2 · S · systemd `service uninstall` requires a live `--user` manager, so headless/SSH/cron cannot remove a unit that `status` still reports.**
`internal/platform/service_linux.go:131-152` — `Uninstall` calls `available(ctx)` and returns its error *before* removing the unit file, while `Status` (:154-188) still reports Installed from the unit-file presence. The launchd path (`service_darwin.go:119-133`) removes the plist unconditionally after a best-effort `bootout`, by contrast. A unit installed while logged in can't be uninstalled over SSH without a session bus, and with linger it may keep running. **Fix:** mirror launchd — remove the unit file even when `systemctl --user` is unreachable (best-effort `disable --now` only when available); note a later-session `daemon-reload` may be needed.

**P7-XP-04 · P2 · S · The ignore matcher is byte-exact (no NFC), so macOS NFD readdir vs NFC patterns break gitignore-parity pruning.**
`internal/ignore/ignore.go:46-62` matches with `filepath.ToSlash` + regex and no Unicode normalization; `internal/scan/scan.go` passes the raw `WalkDir` rel path; `pathkey.Clean` does NFC + fold but only for namespace keys, not ignore. On APFS the filesystem returns NFD (`café` decomposed) while a `.devstrapignore` carries NFC `café/`, so the matcher doesn't prune — a draft bundle ships unexpected content or a junk dir survives, and the same pattern works on a Linux NFC tree, so a fleet diverges. **Fix:** NFC-normalize both pattern bodies (at compile) and match targets (in `Match`/`ShouldPruneDir`), as `pathkey.Clean` does; add NFD/NFC tests. External anchor: the pathkey layer already documents the macOS NFD/NFC hazard; the ignore layer must match it.

**P7-XP-05 · P3 · S · `service status`/`doctor` never validate that the baked ExecPath still exists.**
`service_darwin.go:135-162`/`service_linux.go:154-187` `Status` stat only the plist/unit, and `doctor.go`'s `checkService` reports Installed+Running without parsing `ProgramArguments`/`ExecStart`. After `P7-XP-01`'s brew upgrade (or a manual `rm`), the user sees only "not running," not "ExecPath missing." **Fix:** parse the first argument of the unit and surface "ExecPath missing: …" with a `service install` remedy.

**P7-XP-06 · P3 · S · Ignore matching is always case-sensitive, diverging from git on default macOS (`core.ignorecase=true`) while `path_key` is case-folded.**
`ignore.go` has no `(?i)`/fold; `pathkey.go` folds case for namespace identity. A user porting a gitignore with mixed-case patterns gets different pruning on macOS than git would, and the deliberate fleet-portable case-sensitivity isn't documented in `spec/11`. Primarily a doc fix. **Fix:** document the deliberate case-sensitivity in `spec/11` (preferred) or optionally fold on `darwin`.

**P7-XP-07 · P3 · S · macOS Seatbelt `Available()` is stat-only; Linux backends actually probe.**
`sandbox_darwin.go:31-40` checks only that `/usr/bin/sandbox-exec` exists and is executable, while `sandbox_linux.go` launch-probes bubblewrap and Landlock. A `sandbox-exec` that's present but broken (restricted container, broken SIP image) makes `auto`/`require` believe Seatbelt is available and fail at runtime instead of honestly degrading/refusing at resolve time. Not fail-open (the child is still wrapped and fails). **Fix:** probe `sandbox-exec -p '(version 1)(allow default)' /usr/bin/true` under a short timeout. External anchor: `sandbox-exec` is deprecated-but-relied-on across the industry; a functional probe is the standard hardening (Appendix B, T3).

## Product & Commercial Readiness

**P7-PROD-01 · P2 · M · The two-device pairing ceremony is the onboarding drop-off cliff — 8 commands across two machines with 2 codes and 2 out-of-band fingerprints.**
The solo loop is 7 commands (`README.md:152-161`); the second device — where the whole "identical `~/Code` everywhere" promise finally pays off — requires 8 more ordered commands across both machines plus two pairing-code pastes and two fingerprints read over a separate trusted channel in each direction (`docs/quickstart.md:80-108`; `devices.go`). Drop-off concentrates exactly at value realization. **Fix:** (1) embed the fingerprint in the signed pairing-code blob so one paste carries both (aloud-read becomes optional high-assurance); (2) a guided `devstrap pair` wizard that prints the *other* device's next command and blocks until it observes the peer's enrollment; (3) a `devstrap up` folding init+scan+hub init+sync for device 1 and a `devstrap join <code>` (hub URL carried in the pairing blob) for device 2 — orchestration over shipped commands, no new crypto. Track time-to-second-device-sync as the north-star metric.

**P7-PROD-02 · P2 · L · The zero-knowledge hub has no server-side lifecycle enforcement, so a hosted operator cannot bound a tenant's storage (extends P4-HUB-15).**
`hub_compact.go` requires the client's sealing key to publish a snapshot, and `r2.go:471` `PutBlob` has no size/quota/rate gate. A managed operator holds no keys, so it cannot run `hub compact` or safely GC, and a free-tier client that never compacts grows its prefix without bound while the operator pays the bill. This is the core economic blocker for a hosted tier. **Fix:** move lifecycle enforcement to the storage layer (R2 bucket lifecycle rules; LIST-based per-prefix size caps — object sizes are visible without decryption); gateway quota rejection keyed on the prefix byte-sum (needs the `P7-PROD-04` broker); the retention manifest is a signed *plaintext* head, so the operator can observe each tenant's retention-floor age and nudge laggards without decrypting anything. External anchor: Zero/Litestream expose an explicit, observable purge floor (Appendix B, T2).

**P7-PROD-03 · P2 · M · There is no version-skew policy, so fail-closed snapshot/envelope checks wedge a `brew`-upgraded mixed-version fleet.**
`snapshot.go` rejects a mismatched version fail-closed (:257,280,307,427); `events.go:524-534` drops unknown-envelope events to a durable skip; `encryptedhub.go:272` shows the enc.v1→v2 break required *re-founding the workspace on a fresh hub*. Since devices `brew upgrade` independently, device A bumping the snapshot version and running `hub compact` leaves device B unable to import — silently non-converging — with no in-CLI "your peer needs to upgrade" signal. For a paid product this is support-generating and data-loss-adjacent. **Fix:** adopt and document an N-1 compatibility guarantee (read-support one release before write-support); stamp a producer min-reader version into snapshot/retention objects for an actionable error; add a `doctor --remote` version-skew warning; never repeat a re-found-required break (ship an in-place migrator like `hub migrate-events`).

**P7-PROD-04 · P2 · L · There is no control-plane identity — `workspace_id` is client-minted with no binding to an account, device, or quota (extends P4-SEC-08).**
`hub.go:167` sets `WorkspaceID` from the client-chosen `store.WorkspaceID`; keys are `workspaces/<workspace_id>/…`; there is no account/billing/tenant table anywhere in `internal/` (the Neon control plane in `spec/19:61-68` is documented-not-built), and shared-bucket tenancy rests on client-honored prefixes. To bill, rate-limit, or cap devices, the managed tier must map an authenticated account → workspace(s) → usage; none of that exists, and with a shared credential any client can name any prefix. This is the concrete engineering prerequisite behind `SCALE-*`. **Fix:** build the minimal control plane — an authenticated account service issuing per-device short-lived, prefix-scoped credentials via an STS-style broker (the `P4-SEC-08` enforcement point), `account→workspace_id→device` rows + `billing_state`, and a server-signed account↔workspace binding. All non-secret control metadata; the data plane stays zero-knowledge. External anchor: Tailscale's separate signing authority + synchronous revocation (Appendix B, T4).

**P7-PROD-05 · P3 · M · There is no telemetry seam for the commercial tier's MAU/sync metrics (extends P4-HUB-14).**
Nothing phones home (correct for trust; `spec/02:294`), and there is no client-side event-emit abstraction to attach opt-in analytics to. A business needs MAU and sync frequency; the naive place (the hub) is exactly where zero-knowledge forbids reading. Distinct from `P4-HUB-14` (hub-side op/byte counters). **Fix:** add one client-side opt-in seam (no-op sink by default, disclosed coarse counters, never paths/remotes/content); prefer deriving active-device/active-workspace counts server-side from the `P7-PROD-04` broker's access logs for anything billing-critical.

**P7-PROD-06 · P3 · L · There is no team/org trust surface — org concept, audit-log export, and SSO block company adoption (extends P4-SYNC-08).**
`spec/02:72` names a small engineering team as a secondary persona but `:157-161` lists team admin UI and hosted billing as non-goals; the single-workspace invariant is DB-enforced (`P4-SYNC-08` open); there is no audit-log export and no SSO. **Fix:** split the classes so they can be sequenced — engineering: multi-workspace membership (`P4-SYNC-08`) is the substrate for teams, and `devstrap audit export` is a genuinely-new, S-effort formatter over the already-signed event log; business (defer, gated on `P7-PROD-04`): SSO/SCIM, org billing roles, admin console.

## Appendix B — External research anchors

Gathered 2026-07-10 via exa; each practice is cross-checked against a DevStrap finding.

- **T1 — git as a sync/storage backend.** git-annex treats non-fast-forward push rejection as the normal, retryable outcome of concurrent pushes (journal-first, batch-commit-second); its tracking branch grew to 5 GB vs 40 MB of content without scheduled `repack`/`gc`/`prune`; Terraform-state-in-git consensus is that git is a safe *versioned* store but not a *mutable coordination* store. → `P7-HUB-01/02/03`. (git-annex bug tracker & tips; oneuptime/devops.SE, accessed 2026-07-10.)
- **T2 — CRDT compaction & snapshot exchange.** Automerge-repo compacts to *content-addressed* keys precisely to make concurrent compaction lock-free (validating DevStrap's `snapshot.v1` design) but still needs a conflict-safe "current" pointer update; Replicache warns never to key the sync watermark on client identity (validating per-device Seq cursors); Zero/Litestream expose an explicit observable purge floor. → `P7-SYNC-01`, `P7-PROD-02`. (automerge.org, doc.replicache.dev, rocicorp zero-docs, accessed 2026-07-10.)
- **T3 — OS sandbox hardening.** OpenAI Codex combines bubblewrap + Landlock + seccomp (Landlock-only is a fallback) and documents an explicit bypass rather than silent degradation; Landlock rules inherit only through the applying thread and cap at 16 layers; `sandbox-exec` is deprecated-since-10.8 but relied on by Bazel/Nix/Homebrew/Anthropic/OpenAI, so a functional probe is standard. → `P7-SEC-03`, `P7-XP-07`. (github.com/openai/codex, docs.kernel.org/landlock, accessed 2026-07-10.)
- **T4 — key rotation & revocation in E2EE multi-device systems.** Tailscale's tailnet lock uses a separate signing authority and *synchronous* revocation, with a rotation-signature chain; academic consensus is that group protocols don't achieve full post-compromise security until every member processes the update — so revocation must be forward-only and *durable*, and containment (blob re-encryption) is the urgent part. → `P7-SYNC-01`, `P7-SEC-02`, `P7-SEC-05`. (tailscale.com whitepaper, USENIX/eprint analyses, agenix-rekey, accessed 2026-07-10.)
- **T5 — Apple notarization for Homebrew-distributed Go binaries.** Homebrew 5.0.0 audits codesigning + notarization and removes Gatekeeper-failing casks by **2026-09-01**, with `--no-quarantine` being removed; OpenAI Codex's cask was rejected in production for being signed-but-not-notarized (codesign alone is insufficient); the ephemeral-per-run-keychain CI pattern is the norm. → `P7-QUAL-02/03`. (github.com/Homebrew/brew#20755, openai/codex#23366, goreleaser.com notarize docs, accessed 2026-07-10.)
- **T6 — SQLite-backed local-first reliability.** `cp`-style copies of a live DB are unsafe; the safe options are the Backup API and `VACUUM INTO`, with multiple sources reporting `VACUUM INTO` as more reliable on a hot DB; single-writer pool + busy-timeout is the standard pattern (validating DevStrap's design). → `P7-HUB-05`, `P7-DATA-03` (DevStrap's `db backup --full` already uses `VACUUM INTO`, which is correct). (litestream.io, oldmoe.blog, accessed 2026-07-10.)
